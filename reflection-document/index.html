I had written some observations that were just free-form. Completing a self-interview made me think in a different way. I then looked back at my original notes and edited the interview to include those points, as they were valid.

Maybe this was just a long-winded way to make a decision, but the process taught me a lot about the permaculture design process.

 

Getting frustrated with my Health design, I realised that this was ok because I was using the Design Web, and it's not a linear process. Moving from one anchor point to any other anchor point at any time is part of the framework's strength. I'm just not sure how to show this process in my documentation

 

Something that I find really hard is differentiating the design process from the design itself, so when evaluating for example, I start talking about the design process, rather than the design. 

I have utilised external support by sharing designs with Carla's forum groups - shared my health design and the community structure design

 

For my ALP i think I need to include something about how I don't want the quality of my designs to be affected by being on a tight deadline. I am struggling to get to grips with Functions, Systems and elements, and so in my compost design there is a really good opportunity to learn a lot, and this will serve my other five designs well, so it's good to take the time now, even if it means I miss my deadline...Aaarrgh. 

Also, I feel as though I am letting so much go in order to do this work, missing out on social stuff. Jud needs me and I'm not going to put her welfare behind this diploma.

The whole meta thing drives me potty. I can very easily slide into the meta in a design and start designing the design process. Is there a tool or something to help with this?

For example, I started doing my ALP and then i was evaluating the design process rather than the design - it's very easy to get these in a muddle
