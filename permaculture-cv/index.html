Permaculture CV

Profile

Permaculture had been on my radar since I was eighteen, as someone in the community I lived in back then was growing turnips in a geodesic dome, and I remember the turnip grower saying he was using permaculture methods. I've always loved turnips. 

I thought maybe there was something in this old Permaculture malarkey and in 2018 I did the Permaculture Design Certificate, then signed up for the Diploma in Applied Permaculture, and here we are. 

Now I want to integrate it into my daily life, work and everything. Including turnip growing.

Farm/Project/garden visits

Ashton Community Walled Garden Preston | September 2021
Visited to inform my Community Gardens Structure design. I saw how complicated it is to run a community garden, how personalities present certain challenges, how working with a board presents issues. A fabulous project.

Living Potential Care Farm Wetherby | September 2021
Attended as part of the Land Workers Alliance Northern Region farm visit programme. A slick operation and doing hugely beneficial work. Impressive stuff.

Bowling Community Orchard
I volunteered here for a couple of days to see how an orchard project is run. The Apple Day was a wonderful event. These guys have been going for a long time and really know what they're doing. The orchard is heavenly.

Kitchen Garden Tour
In 2018 I was awarded an RHS bursary to make a tour of prestigious kitchen gardens. What a time we had! You can read about it here: Kitchen Garden tour 

Le Manoir aux Quat'Saisons
As part of the kitchen garden tour I completed a week's work experience at Raymond Blanc's incredible kitchen garden. The highlight was standing in the kitchen eating quite a lot of mind-blowingly delicious food. I thought my heart was going to burst with gratitude. And the gardening team were the loveliest bunch of people you could wish to meet. 

The Seed Co-operative
Myself and my partner completed a one-week work trial at the Seed Cooperative as we were applying for a job there. Sadly, we didn’t get the job, but it was a fascinating experience and we had a great time there. The highlight for me was using the incredible spinning seed sorter contraption. They let me use it, which made me very happy!
