Framework

Client interview, SMART goals, strengths and weaknesses analysis.

Resources/support

Killian's web design skills, other apprentice's websites for inspiration, Miro, Google Forms, Word, PowerPoint.

Context

This is a design about how I'm presenting my diploma portfolio for assessment and for a wider audience.

Having had some trouble presenting designs to people in the past, and this having quite serious consequences for the projects, I want to make sure that my designs are communicable to as many people as possible. This design will help me decide what format to use and how to approach it. I have now booked my assessment dates for my portfolio (the IPA and FPA), and this made me realise that I needed to think carefully about how to present this body of work. 

I enjoy looking at other diploma students' design portfolios and I would like to share my own, but would only be happy to do so if my work was of a standard that I was happy with, in  a format that I'm happy with.

Goals

I used SMART goals to pinpoint what I wanted. I created this with a Miro template.

Observation

I used a tool called Client Interview to find out what I was wanting to get out of my portfolio design. Yes, I interviewed myself.

This helped me think about how I want my website to look and who it is for. It also allowed me to see that accessibility, engagement and clarity are my priorities.

Boundaries

Time is a boundary, I don't have time to be faffing - I just need a simple format that each design fits into for presentation. 

The fact that the designs need to be clear and concise, accessible and engaging is a boundary, because it means that I can't ramble. I need to be careful that I don't drift off subject. 

I don't want to spend a lot of money.

Analysis

I use Miro for a lot of my design work. It's an online whiteboard platform that is designed to allow teams to collaborate. Boards seem to be able to expand into infinity,  it's a very visual tool, and as my thinking is non-linear, it helps me to keep track of my ideas. I find it indispensable for designing. Because I love it so much, I wondered if I could use it to present my portfolio.

I did a strengths and weaknesses analysis to get a better picture;

It was quite clear that a website would be better. I hadn't even considered that my partner could make me a website. That is how fixated I was on using Miro. If anyone wants to get me a Christmas/birthday present, subscription to Miro? Just sayin'...

Design Decisions

I will;

  • present my portfolio on a website
  • use different methods for creating content - including Miro, Word and PowerPoint, as well as others, but then each design will be displayed in same format on the website
  • continue working on designs and add them to the website when they're complete
  • write a website brief
  • set a writing style with rules

Evaluation

Have I met my goals?
1. Work out how to present my diploma
Yes, I have definitely done this. I have a template for the presentation of each of my designs. The design process worked well to meet this goal as it helped me to see things from different perspectives. It meets my long and short term needs because I now have a website to present my portfolio and I can add a section for my gardening business. I didn't quite keep to the deadline but I'm still pleased with the turnaround.  As you can see, the website is up and running!

I wanted to have a low carbon website. One option for this was to go with a low carbon website company. However, website designers aim to create a fast, efficient websites to  achieve high performance. Low carbon is a by-product of this. For this reason, I feel that I have met my goal. 

2. Do a speedy design
I had given myself a day to complete it, and I did whizz through it, but I had to go back and do a lot of tweaking because I'd missed stuff out. Even though I missed my deadline, it served my need to get a design done quickly, to progress the project. It helped me keep focussed and concise. 

3. Make it visually engaging
I will have to wait for feedback, but personally, I like it! 

4. Allow anyone to understand my designs
Again, I will have to wait until I get some feedback

Feedback

I gathered feedback using this Google Form. Please feel free to fill it in if you would like to.

The feedback was mostly very positive and there were some excellent suggestions, some of which I have already implemented. Some of the points were things that I have planned to do, but haven't had time to do, mostly suggestions about improving images, adding more images and making the images the appropriate size. 

This was a very interesting part of the design process. I started to understand the limitations of feedback via a Google Forms,  and had to tweak my questions to get a clearer understanding. But now I am evaluating my evaluation process! There is a whole design project in designing a website and another one in gathering feedback.

Permaculture Principles


Strategic Principles

Feed what you want to grow
I would like permaculture to grow - hopefully my website will help people to understand what it is, and inspire them to incorporate it into their own lives.
Eventually, I want to grow the website into a platform for my business, so I'm creating something that will serve my short term and long term goals.
I also really love techy stuff, and this design process will increase my skill set.
Use edges
Clients looking for garden design will also see my permaculture designs, and therefore may learn something about permaculture and want to know more, perhaps even have it used in their gardens.
Make it beautiful
I intend to create a visually pleasing and cohesive website. 
Use onsite resources
Killian! I hadn't thought of enlisting his help, as I usually enjoy building websites myself, but as he offered and I'm short of time, I decided to run with it.
Work Where it Counts 
Rather than creating a second-rate presentation on Miro, developing the website idea is putting energy where it is most beneficial, and most accessible.
Invest Wisely
I am investing money into my website, but it is a sound investment as it will also support my business in the future. I am also using my time wisely, because trying to make Miro be something it isn't actually designed for would be a lot of work.
Be Realistic
Trying to get Miro to do everything was unrealistic, and also, I need to be realistic about how much time and energy I have for doing this work

Ethics

People Care
Self care; I'm making my diploma journey smoother by creating a well-thought out presentation process, This should mean that I experience as little stress as possible in the run up to completing my portfolio.
Earth Care
I intend to have a website that has a low carbon footprint.
Fair Share
Sharing designs with people online, widening the understanding of Permaculture.

Reflection on the design process

What I learnt about permaculture through the design process
I did a SWOC analysis but it didn't work, the subject was too basic a concept so I just did a strengths and weaknesses analysis instead, which was appropriate to the task. That was really useful to see, I am starting to see how the finding the right tool for the right task is important.

What I learnt about myself through the design process
I genuinely had not considered getting Killian to set up a website for me, even though it may have been obvious to anyone else. Through the process I let go of my fixation with Miro, and with my need to do everything and conceded that I ought to let him help me! I have seen that I have blind spots. You may call it stubbornness... 

How this design process has helped me:
It has helped me by giving me a place to put all my designs. An unexpected benefit is that it has given me a outlet for my creativity and my love of techy stuff. 

I learnt some new skills, such as how to make documents more accessible. 

What went well?
I made good use of the tools. Overall, I was pleased with the design process. I didn't have time to go off on tangents and managed to be quite methodical. 

What was challenging?
Choosing the right tools and understanding which elements of the design process fitted into which design step. Knowing which elements of the design fit under which heading. I have paralysis about analysis.

It's also difficult to separate the design process and the design itself

Long term vision and goals
I hope to have my full portfolio of ten designs uploaded onto the website in time for my final assessment, which is on the twenty-second of February, 2022. 

At that stage, I can begin to develop the website in order to promote my gardening business and to also present the design work that I will do after the accreditation. 

I would like to help other diploma students with their websites, and by that I mean get Killian to help them with their websites!
