Context

For the Diploma in Applied Permaculture Design, I must complete ten designs. In order to facilitate this process, I have created an Action Learning Pathway, and this is the design for it. It is a map of my learning intentions/aspirations and an exploration into why I am doing the diploma and what I hope to gain from it.

Vision

Allow yourself to dream and create goals

I used this anchor point to think about what I hoped to gain from doing the diploma and also more broadly about how I want to live my life.

The Give Back Generation

I have been resistant to putting this into the world, because it may seem a bit worthy, but it really is at the core of my diploma and my life. Permaculture to me is Buddhist practice put into practice! It's about Earth Care, People Care and Fair share, all ethics that fit within the teachings of the Buddha, but with Permaculture they have practical application. The Give Back Generation is about recognising that you already have enough, and that it's time to give back. My teacher Burgs explains it here.

I wrote a timeline of my life, and then used this to create a 'river of life' drawing'. Sounds a bit hippy to me but it's a real permaculture tradition. I did enjoy seeing the representation of my life on paper. It is too personal to share here, and the past is the past. Suffice to say, like everyone. I've had my ups and downs. It helped me to see the different phases of my life and to see that I am now in a phase where I take responsibility for my own actions and would like to make a conscious effort to shape my life into the best it can be.  'Energy flows where attention goes' and I would like to use my diploma to channel my attention into the things that really matter, to put some really good bends in the river and manage any upcoming rapids so that they don't capsize me. That's enough of that metaphor. 

To get an idea of where I am at now, have a look at my Permaculture CV.

Aims

I would like to finish the diploma with a deep understanding of a wide range of permaculture frameworks, processes, tools and approaches.

I'd like to get to the stage where I can incorporate these into my garden design work, my business, my relationships and all aspects of my life.

I would like to create a portfolio that has a diverse range of applications, such as land stewardship, health and wellbeing, community development etc. However, my main focus will be land stewardship.

Community is often at the heart of permaculture projects. I must say that this is something that I find very challenging but I am always drawn to community projects. I hope to find solutions for this aspect of my work. 

I hope to complete my accreditation in August 2022. 

I would like to create a portfolio of designs that help me with my aspirations as a Buddhist.

Patterns

Identify helpful and unhelpful patterns
I haven't indicated which of the following patterns are helpful and which are unhelpful, because they can all be either. The best approach for me is to be aware of these habits and utilise them to my own benefit. 

  • I obsess over details Excellent, details are important at times. But when focussing on details becomes a way of procrastinating, a tight deadline prevents me from getting waylaid.
  • I am a perfectionist Excellent, perfection is important at times. But when wanting perfection becomes a way of procrastinating, the phrase 'good enough is good enough' is useful.
  • I am non-linear in my thinking so bounce around with different ideas and go off on tangents Miro is great for this, I can put all my thoughts in an appropriate place.
  • When I am determined and motivated, I can chip away at something and stay focussed Flow is a lovely state to be in, but difficult to get back into once it's broken. I will keep phone on silent and stay off social media when studying. 
  • I work well with short deadlines (I rock the lastminute.com method) and structure Keep the timeline as a guide but work realistically and with self-compassion
  • I am a visual learner Miro! Pinterest! Website! 

In 2018 I completed the RHS Level 3 Diploma in Applied Horticulture with distinction. I also have a BA honours degree. So I know I can study and I know how I study; I leave things to the last moment and then work fixatedly.

Principles

Everything is connected

 

All the designs are connected in many ways, some support all of the others, earlier ones support those that follow, as I develop my learning. And as I generally have had more than one design in progress at any time, they influence each other as they progress.
This design is key at making sure that those connections are recognised and exploited.

Nature moves in circles

Learning cycle – organic flow. The more you learn, the more you don’t know!

Energy is abundant but not unlimited

Reuse, recycle. Borrowing from other diploma student's diplomas, getting inspiration from other people’s ALPs

Do more with less

Stacking, make elements serve more than one function – The repurposing of my website is a good way of stacking. I would like an interface to present my designs, I’d like to write about the process, and I’d like to get into writing again, so writing up my designs will get me started.

Use on-site and local resources whenever possible

Use skills I already have, and ask friends/networks to help when stuck.

Don’t buy books, borrow.

If it ain't broke, don't fix it

Make do with what you’ve got. I’m not going to try to do a design for something way off in the future, but design for what’s in front of me instead. Mostly…I would like to do a bit ‘dream set up’ design – i.e moving to Ireland and getting land with a collective.

I will design for now.

Resilience is true security

Diverse sources of help – forum and guild, Facebook diploma group and Permaculture Official UK

Using range of principles, frameworks, tools etc.

Make mistakes, start slow and small and tweak away. I tried using a Gantt chart but it was too complicated, so I used a basic chart. Maybe this will turn out to be too basic but it’s an experiment.

Design for catastrophe – what if I have to get a full-time job and can’t find time to do my designs? How do I design for that? Having an ALP gives me a road map through my diploma, so I will already have assessed the risks, challenges etc.

First things first; doing the ALP before doing any more designs is setting a strong foundation for the rest of my diploma. Yay!

Take responsibility

The cornerstone of the Buddha's teachings, this is what the law of Karma teaches us.  With my ALP I would like to focus on designs that will help me grow, and to bring about positive change. I will try not to ‘make war on what I don’t want’ but rather design for what can be improved

Get some! Obtain a yield! 

I will decide the yield I want and plan for it. My yield will be a cohesive portfolio of designs, new skills and knowledge.

 

Creativity is an unlimited resourceCreative thinking is the best way of finding solutions and this design will help me find new ways of being creative, of coming up with new tools, new perspectives and new approaches.

Ethics

People Care is met because I am designing a life for myself that is fulfilling, healthy and compassionate. As my core aim is to be part of the Give Back Generation, care for other people is integral to the design.

Earth Care is met because part of being in the Give Back Generation is to tread lightly, to reduce what I take out. Working with a deep connection with nature is integral to this.

Future Care is met because, as part of the Give Back Generation, I will be taking out less and hopefully having a positive effect on the future of the planet.

Evaluate

Evaluate progress

In the process of writing this design, I did quite a bit of waffly evaluation, which I won't subject anyone to. So I'm going to use the De Bono's Thinking Hats tool to summarise.

I'd like to look at each design I've chosen and see how they correlate with my overall objectives.

  • live lightly
  • develop my connection to nature
  • nurture relationships
  • let go of that which doesn't serve me
  • give back
  • be happy
  • nurture creativity

Design 1. Portfolio Presentation
This facilitates the presentation and creation of my permaculture diploma so indirectly, it meets all of my aspirations. It directly meets my aspiration to nurture creativity and to give back. And it did make me very happy!

Design 2. Kitchen Garden 
My aspiration to develop my connection with nature is met by this design. The process nurtured my creativity and had it been implemented, would have helped me meet the others. Funnily enough, the process did help me 'let go of that which doesn't serve me', because I realised that the project wasn't for me and I needed to walk away from it.

Design 3. Action Learning Pathway
As per my first design, this one supports all of my aspirations, rather than meeting them directly. The aspiration to nurture creativity was met directly though.

Design 4. Being Well
'Letting go of that which doesn't serve me' was the main focus of this design. developing my connection to nature was an important factor too and my other aspirations were supported by it indirectly.

Design 5. Compost
The key aspiration for this design was to 'give back'. Giving back to the soil, regenerating soil life and soil structure - this is very important to me and it's important to remember that giving back isn't just about helping humans.

Although none of the designs will directly meet all of my aspirations, they should all meet at least one of them, support them all indirectly and definitely not hinder them in any way.

Tweak

I have just hit an impasse with my next design, and I realised that I don't have anything in my ALP about what to do if this happens. I would like to make a flowchart so that I have a process for this situation, to avoid having a big delay and getting behind schedule.

I will return to this design throughout the process of creating my final five designs, so there may well be a ALP part two!
